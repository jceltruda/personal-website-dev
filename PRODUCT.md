# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech recruiters and hiring managers as the primary audience, skimming quickly to decide whether Joseph Celtruda is worth an interview for software-engineering and applied-AI roles. They arrive from a resume link, LinkedIn, or a referral, often on a first pass through many candidates. Research-adjacent readers (professors, labs) are a secondary audience who treat the recommender-systems / AI-optimization work as a credibility signal. The job to be done: let a busy reader grasp who Joseph is, what he has built, and why he is credible — fast — and give them an easy next step (resume, the "Ask about Joseph" AI assistant, or a contact link).

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Joseph Celtruda (M.S. Computer Science @ RPI). It exists to convert a quick skim into an interview by presenting his experience, projects, and skills with clarity and craft, backed by an AI chat assistant grounded in his background that lets visitors ask questions directly. Success looks like a recruiter leaving with a clear, favorable impression and taking a next step (saving the resume, reaching out, or trying the assistant). The site itself is a portfolio piece: its quality is evidence of his engineering taste.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, modern, and technical — three words that hold in tension on purpose. Polished and human enough to feel friendly (the conversational AI assistant reinforces this), but precise and engineered enough to read as someone who ships clean systems. Not minimalist-for-its-own-sake: personality, warmth, and a few deliberate moments of delight are welcome, as long as they never undercut clarity or credibility. Confident without being loud. The voice is direct and first-person, never corporate.

## Anti-references

- **Generic developer-portfolio template** — gradient hero, skill-percentage bars, a cookie-cutter identical-card grid, the interchangeable starter look. The single biggest thing to avoid.
- **Corporate / SaaS marketing** — navy-and-gradient palettes, feature-card grids, the big-hero-metric template, stocky enterprise polish.
- **Loud / over-animated** — excessive motion, neon glow, gimmicky scroll effects, anything that distracts from the content.
- **Cluttered / busy** — dense, overloaded layouts with competing elements and weak hierarchy.

## Design Principles

- **Evidence over adjectives.** Show the work and let specifics (companies, projects, results) carry credibility; don't lean on buzzwords or self-description.
- **Respect the skim.** Optimize for a fast first pass — strong hierarchy, scannable sections, an obvious next step. A recruiter should "get it" in seconds.
- **The site is the proof.** Craft, performance, and attention to detail are themselves the argument that Joseph builds quality software. Practice what you preach.
- **Warmth without noise.** Personality and delight are allowed and encouraged, but every flourish must earn its place and never compete with the content.
- **Confident, not loud.** Make deliberate, opinionated choices instead of safe defaults — but express confidence through restraint and precision, not volume.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Not a primary focus, but keep sensible defaults: legible text contrast (aim for WCAG AA, ~4.5:1 on body text), keyboard-reachable interactive elements, semantic HTML, and the existing `prefers-reduced-motion` handling on scroll reveals. Don't over-invest beyond reasonable, low-cost good practice.
